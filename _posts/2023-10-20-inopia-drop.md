Following the cancellation of a collaborative opportunity professor originally organized by Dr Robert Toft, students Aiden Wiebe and Emmanuella Stratas create a record label called Mistapes, which they structure as a music industry club at the University of Western Ontario.  Mistapes partners Aiden Wiebe with Sam Hansell, which results in "Inopia", Sam Hansell's solo debut.  Sitting at 49 minutes and featuring instrumental performances from a dozen students, "Inopia" released with a sold-out show at The Poacher's Arms.  The merch booth was emptied before the next day.
